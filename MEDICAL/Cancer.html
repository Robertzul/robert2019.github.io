CANCER

CANCER is anything that may cause a normal body cell to develop abnormally protentially can cause Cancer.
General categories of cancer-related or causative agents are as follows:
chemical or toxic compound exposures, ioniing radiation, some pathoges, and human genetics.

OR

CANCER is the catchall term applied to disease caused by the uncotrolled growth of abnomal cells and invade other tissues.
Cells become cancerous due to the accumulation of defects, mutations, in their DNA.

CANCER isn't one disease. It is many different disease, more than 100 and counting.

Each kind of Cancer is usually named for the cell type in wich begins.
Cancer that starts in a lung is called lung cancer; cancer that starts in pigment cells in the skin, which are known as melanocytes, is called melanoma.

When detected and treated early, cancer can often be stooped.
Cancer is a leading cause of death and disability around the world.

CANCER SYMPTOMS

Cancer symptoms and signs depend on the specific type and grade of cancer;
Although general signs and symtoms are not very specific the following can be found in patients with different cancers:
fatigue, weight loss, pain, skin changes, change in bowel or bladder function, unusual bleeding, persistent cough or voice change, fever, lumps, or tissue masses.

TREATMENT

Treatment protocols vary according to the type and stage of the cancer.Most treatment protocols are designed to fit the indiviual patient's disease.
However, most treatments include at least one of the following and may include all: surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy

CAUSES


1. Infections
2. Inherited genetic defects (for example, BRCA1 and BRCA2 mutations)
3. Environmental factors (for example, air pollution)
4. Poor lifesyle choice -- such as smoking and
heavy alcohol use -- can also damage DNA and lead to cancer.
