BLOOD CANCER

most Blood cancers , also called hematologic cancer, start in the bone marrow, which is where blood is produced.
Blood cancer occur when abnormal blood cells start growing out of control, interrupting the fuction of normal blood cells, which fight off infection and produce new blood cells.

SIGNS OF BLOOD CANCER

Several kings of cancer attack the cells that make up your blood.
Their symptoms usually come on slowly, so you might not even notice them. And some people have no symptoms at all.

But there are few thing to look for with the most common kinds of blood cancer.

TYPES OF BLOOD CANCERS

The three main types of blood cancer are leukemia, lymphoma and myeloma

LEUKEMIA

Blood cells are made inside your bone marrow, and that's where leukemia starts.
It cases your body to make white blood cells that grow out of control and live longer than they're supposed to.
And unlike normal white blood cells, they don't help your fight infection.

There are many diffent forms of leukemia Some get worse quickly (acute).
You'll probably feel very sick very suddenly,like you've come down with the flu.
Other forms can take years to cause symptoms (chronic).
your frist clue may be abnormal results on a routine blood test.

Most signs of leukemia happen becauses the cancer cells keep your healthy blood cells from growing and working normally.

Anemia: This is when your body doesn't make enough red blood cells, or the ones you have don't do their jobs well
Signs of it include:


1. Feeling tired and weak
2. Dizziness
3. Pale skin
4. Chest pain
5. Shortness of breath

Pool clotting: platelets are the cells that make your blood clot.
When your body doesn't make enough of them, small cuts may bleed more than usual, or you might have a bloody nose often.
You may also have


1. Unusual bruising
2. Bleeding gums
3. Tiny red on your skin from broken blood vessels
4. Heavy periods
5. Bowel movements that are black or straked with red.

Other Symptoms: Because your white blood cells don't fight infection well, you'll get sick more often and take longer to get over it.
You may get a lot of fevers and have night sweats.

Cancer cell can build up in your lymph nodes, tonsils, liver, and spleen and cause them to swell.
You may feel lumps in your neck or armpit, or you may feel full after only eating a small amount.
You may lose a lot of weight without tring.
And the growth of cancer cells in your bone marrow sometimes cause bone pain.

LYMPHOMA

Your lymph system carries infection-fighting white blood cells called lymphocytes throughout your body and helps to get rid of waste.
Lymphoma causes your body to make lymphocytes that grow out of control and it harder for you to fight infection.

Swollen lymph nodes are the main sign of lymphoma. You may notice a lump in your neck, armpit, or groin.
Lymph nodes father inside your body may press on your organs and cause coughing, shortness of breath, or pain in your chest, belly, or bones. Your spleen may get bigger, making you full or bloated. The swollen nodes aren't usually painful, but they may hurt when you drink alcohol.

COMMON SIGNS OF LYMPHOMA are:

1. Fever
2. Night sweats
3. Feeling tired
4. Unexplained weight loss
5. Itchy skin

MULTIPLE MYELOMA

The plasma cell is another type of disease-fighting cell in your bloodstream.
Multiple myeloma causes your bone marrow to make plasma cells that grow out of control and keep your from making enough healthy blood cells.
They also release chemical into your blood that can hurt your orgains and tissues.
Some forms get worse faster than others, but symptoms typically don't show up untill you've had it for a while.

BONE PAIN The most common sign of multiple myeloma is serious and long-lasting pain, usually in your back or ribs.
The cancer cells release a chemical that stops the normal growth and healing process in your bones. they get thin and weak and can break easily.

Damage to the bones in your spine can put pressure on your nervers and cause pain or weakness in your legs, tingling in your arms, and loss of bowel or bladder control.

Hypercalcemia: Multiple myeloma causes high levels of calcium in your blood.
That can lead to:


1. Nausea and stomach pain
2. Excessive thirst and urination
3. Constipation
4. Loss of appetite
5.weakness

Blood cancer treatment and therapy options

Treatment for blood cancer depends on the type of cancer, your age, how fast the cancer is progressing, where the cancer has spread and other factors.
Some common blood cancer treatment include:

Stem cell transplantation :A stem cell transplant infuses healthy blood-forming stem cells into the body.
Stem cell may be collected from the bone marrow, circulating blood and umbilical cord blood.

Chemotherapy: Chemotherapy uses anticancer drugs to interfere with and stop the growth of cancer cells in the body.
Chemotherapy for blood cancer sometimes involves giving several drugs. together in a set regimen.
This treatment may also be given before a stem cell transplant.

Radiation therapy: Radiation therapy may be used to destroy cancer cells or to relieve pain or discomfort.
It may be also be given before a stem cell transplant.
