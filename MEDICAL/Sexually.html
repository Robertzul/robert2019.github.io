SEXUALLY TRANSMITTED DISEASES

Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STDs) are infections that pass from one person to another through sexually contact.
They are also known as Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs) Or Venereal Diseases (VD).
... Infectious organisms can also move between people in semen,
vaginal secretions, or blood during sexual intercourse.
Some STDs can spread through the use of unsterilized drug needles, from mother to infant during childbirth or breast-feeding, and blod transfusions.

PREVENTION

CONDOM

CONDOM: can help prevent the spread of STDs.
Sex using a Condom is the safest way to prevent the spread of STDs.
Condoms are known as barrier contraceptives, due to they presentation of a physical barrier to microbes.
For each oral, vaginal, or anal sex act use a new latex condoms.

ABSTINENCE

ABSTINENCE: Abstaining from any sexual act is the most effective way to avoid an STD.

MONOGAMY TOONE UNINFECTED PARTNER

MONOGAMY TOONE UNINFECTED PARTNER: A long term, monogamous relationship with one persecute who is not infected can reduce the risk of contracting an STD.

CHECK FOR INFECTIONS

CHECK FOR INFECTIONS: Before sexual intercourses with a new partner , check that the partner and yourself have no STDs.

DRINK ALCOHOL IN MODERATION

People who have consumed too much alcohol are more likely to engage in risky behavior. Avoid using recreational drugs, which may also affect judgment
